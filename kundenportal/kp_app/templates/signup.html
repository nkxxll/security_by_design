Why are there no password rules?...

Creating password rules with a specific password length requirement but avoiding other strict criteria can indeed increase password entropy and make your system more secure. However, it's essential to strike a balance between security and user convenience. Here's why you might choose not to specify additional password rules when enforcing a 10-character length:

  • User-Friendly Approach: Many users find strict password rules cumbersome and frustrating. By only requiring a 10-character minimum length, you strike a balance between security and user-friendliness, making it easier for individuals to create and remember their passwords.
  • Diverse Passwords: With a focus on length rather than a complex mix of character types, users may naturally create more diverse passwords. Longer passwords are typically harder to guess, and they may include a variety of characters, further enhancing security.
  • Less Risk of Forgotten Passwords: Complex rules can lead to forgotten passwords, which often results in users having to reset their passwords or contact support. By keeping it simple with a 10-character minimum, users are less likely to forget their passwords, reducing the need for password recovery.
  • Resilience Against Dictionary Attacks: While not specifying rules like requiring special characters or numbers may make brute force attacks more manageable, it also makes dictionary attacks less effective. Longer passwords that don't follow a specific pattern can be significantly more resistant to dictionary-based cracking attempts.
  • Adaptability: Users often use the same passwords across multiple accounts. By allowing them to choose their preferred 10-character combination, you encourage them to create unique passwords for your system, increasing security across the board.
  • Educational Value: When users are allowed to create their own passwords, they may become more aware of what constitutes a strong password. This educational aspect can lead to better password practices beyond your system.
  • Reduced Burden on Support: Complex password rules often lead to users seeking help when they can't create or remember their passwords. By simplifying your requirements, you reduce the burden on your support team.
  • Customization: Some users may have their unique strategies for creating strong and memorable passwords. Allowing them to choose their own password format empowers them to use their methods while still adhering to a minimum length requirement.

While specific password rules can increase security, opting for a 10-character minimum length without further restrictions can be a pragmatic choice, striking a balance between security and user experience. It encourages users to create strong and diverse passwords while also reducing the potential for frustrating experiences and support inquiries.
